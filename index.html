Publications
Site (home page) Link What problem does this article solve? Why does the design suit the task?
www.thewitcher.com The Witcher 3 Next-Gen Update The publication allows the user to find out exactly when he should wait for an update to his platform and what changes will be. On the one hand, the emphasis on the page is only on the list itself, without additional distracting elements, which is proof. But the list itself is poorly formatted - due to a large amount of text, the exact update date for specific platforms is not visible, which is why you have to read everything. Because the whole article is heavily filled with text - it's difficult to quickly read key-changes for the user.
www.diablo4.blizzard.com Dibalo IV Classes Introduces the player to the classes available in the game for a more complete immersion in the atmosphere or to select a class to play in advance. With the help of tabs, it makes it possible to quickly understand what classes are waiting for him in the game, and amazing graphics with a large display of the representative of the class already at the stage of the site section immerses you in the atmosphere of the game. Coins: tabs with classes go beyond the viewport border, especially when switching between them, because the content inside the tab pushes the tabs lower - and you need to constantly scroll back and forth.
www.mundfish.com Atomic Heart Lore Introduces the player to the chronology of events in the game and the game era. Large headings with dates immediately make it clear that we are waiting for the chronology of events. Illustrations emphasize the atmosphere that is described in the lore.
www.hogwartslegacy.com Hogwarts Legacy FAQ The faq page so that a user doesn't have to look for answers all over the site. Clear separation of questions and answers from each other due to vignettes between blocks and different styles of the text of the question and answer itself. I would do the alignment not in the center, but on the right, since there is a lot of text in the faq, and it is more difficult to read centered text due to torn lines from both edges.
www.back4blood.com Back 4 Blood Update The news page introduces the user to the new trailer and contains a brief faq. On the one hand, the typical structure of a news page is familiar to the user eye. On the other hand, the trailer could have been inserted not as a link, but as a direct video from YouTube. The FAQ is designed as a bullet list, which is confusing - you need to remove the markers, make the questions bold, and leave the answers as the rest of the text - so the user will immediately understand that it is a faq in front of him and not a list of something random.
